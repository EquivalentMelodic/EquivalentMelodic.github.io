Abstract

This study is going to change the opinion of “buildings,” it encompasses technology along with old ways of construction. Those implementations have the potential to solve major destructive problems and tackle global threats. Egypt suffers from major challenges that hinder its growth and advancement. Amongst those challenges are climate change, wastes and recycling, and a lack of a technological environment. The agricultural sector is by far the most affected sector by those challenges, where many crops get wasted due to short shelf life that is caused by environmental factors, leading to less food availability, and causing a less supply-and-demand ratio. “Modern challenges require innovative solutions.” A quote that best reflects the chosen solution for the mentioned challenge. It is a fruits and vegetables shop made from a composite of mud and hay, called “Adobe.” It will be monitored by sensors and smart systems that track certain environmental features. Those sensors are connected to actuators that turn on whenever there is an unpleasing change. The solution must come up to certain design criteria: low temperature (at least lower by 3 degrees Celsius than its environment); natural, eco-friendly building materials; the utilization of clean, renewable energy sources; and overall building safety. The solution was a massive success, with it demolishing the requirements and passing every test. It promotes crop health and longer shelf life, as well as safety and higher air quality. It presented a modern and innovative way of critical thinking and problem-solving strategies that cope with current challenges.
